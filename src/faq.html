Why are French wines named after the region and not the grape?

Wines in France - really in almost all of Europe - had developed their styles over the centuries; a grape (more often a blend of grapes) was eventually found to suit an area - or even better, express the area. To know where the grapes are grown is to know the style of the wine. To know much about the grapes themselves was wine geekdom, only necessary for professionals.
